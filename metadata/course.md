# CHEM320 - Chemistry of Macromolecules

## Description

This course introduces polymer chemistry as a structure-processing-property discipline. It begins with polymer definitions, molar mass, degree of polymerization, historical development, and environmental motivation; then moves through architecture, stereochemistry, copolymer composition, molar-mass distributions, and the two major polymerization families. The central synthesis sequence covers step-growth polymerization, chain-growth polymerization, precision/controlled methods, macromolecular engineering, and depolymerization/recycling by design. The final sequence connects molecular structure to single-chain conformation, polymer solutions and mixtures, molar-mass characterization, glass transition, and crystallinity. Viscoelasticity and rubber elasticity are treated only as optional instructor/advanced supplements.

## Objectives

By the end of the guide, learners should be able to:

- Define polymer, macromolecule, oligomer, molar mass, degree of polymerization, dispersity, architecture, tacticity, and conformation using current polymer terminology.
- Explain how repeat-unit structure, chain length, stereochemistry, branching, crosslinking, copolymer sequence, and molar-mass distribution affect polymer properties.
- Distinguish thermoplastics, thermosets, elastomers, semicrystalline polymers, amorphous polymers, blends, and copolymers using molecular structure as evidence.
- Calculate number-average and weight-average molar mass, dispersity, and degree of polymerization from discrete distributions or polymerization models.
- Compare step-growth and chain-growth polymerization in terms of reactive species, kinetics, molar-mass buildup, stoichiometric constraints, molecular-weight distribution, and product examples.
- Apply Carothers-type reasoning, stoichiometric imbalance corrections, and gelation concepts to step-growth and network-forming systems.
- Analyze radical, anionic, cationic, coordination, ring-opening, and ring-opening metathesis polymerizations by monomer compatibility, active center, initiation, propagation, termination/transfer, kinetics, and industrial relevance.
- Explain how living/controlled polymerization, reversible-deactivation radical polymerization, single-site catalysis, click/supramolecular chemistry, dendrimers, and macromolecular engineering enable more precise structures.
- Use thermodynamics to reason about polymerization/depolymerization, ceiling temperature, chemical recycling to monomer, biodegradation, and the design criteria for recyclable polymers.
- Model single-chain conformation with ideal-chain and self-avoiding-chain ideas, including end-to-end distance, radius of gyration, scaling laws, and solvent/excluded-volume effects.
- Apply Flory-Huggins-style reasoning to polymer mixing, solvent quality, phase behavior, viscosity, osmotic pressure, light scattering, and GPC/SEC characterization.
- Relate glass transition and crystallinity to molecular motion, morphology, thermal behavior, and practical material selection.
- Select and interpret polymer characterization methods, including molar-mass averages from GPC/SEC and scattering, solution viscosity, thermal transitions by DSC/DMA, and crystallinity by DSC/XRD/density; mechanical/rheological interpretation belongs to the optional Ch09 supplement unless adopted as required scope.
- Evaluate polymer-waste problems and proposed solutions without treating durability, biodegradability, recyclability, and performance as interchangeable goals.
