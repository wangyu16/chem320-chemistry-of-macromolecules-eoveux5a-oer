**Question 2.1** A sample of synthetic polystyrene has a total molecular weight (molar mass) of $104,000\text{ g/mol}$.

- **Task A**: Identify the chemical structure or molecular formula of the repeating monomer unit of polystyrene based on the guide's introductory image. (Hint: The monomer is styrene, $\ce{C8H8}$).
- **Task B**: Calculate the molecular weight of the repeat unit (Atomic weights: $\ce{C} = 12\text{ g/mol}$, $\ce{H} = 1\text{ g/mol}$).
- **Task C:** Calculate the Degree of Polymerization ($\text{DP}$) of this polystyrene sample using the formula:

$$\text{DP} = \frac{\text{Polymer M.W.}}{\text{Repeat Unit M.W.}}$$
